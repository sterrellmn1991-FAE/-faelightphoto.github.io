# Fae Light Photography
### Steve Terrell · Nature · Landscape · Wildlife · Golden Hour

---

A portfolio site for **Fae Light Photography** — a nature and landscape photography practice built around a ground-level perspective inspired by the imagined viewpoint of the fae: low, close, patient, and attuned to the details the standing world walks past.

Every image is shot from below the ordinary line of sight — moss, root systems, morning light through blades of grass, wildlife at eye level. The result is a body of work that treats the natural world not as scenery, but as a subject with its own scale and dignity.

## About the Name

*Fae Light* refers to the quality of light that exists at the edges of the day — the few minutes before sunrise, the long gold of late afternoon, the blue that settles just after sunset. It is also a reference to the perspective the work is shot from: low to the ground, close to the moss, at the level where the small things are large.
